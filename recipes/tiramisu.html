Tiramisu Recipe

Tiramisu is a classic, no-bake Italian dessert featuring layers of coffee-soaked ladyfingers (savoiardi) and a rich, creamy mascarpone mixture. To prepare the filling, egg yolks are traditionally whisked with sugar until pale and thick, then combined with mascarpone cheese; many modern recipes also incorporate whipped cream or whipped egg whites to create a lighter, fluffier texture. The ladyfingers are briefly dipped into cold, strong espresso—sometimes mixed with alcohol like rum or Marsala wine—and arranged in a dish, followed by a layer of the cream mixture, with the process usually repeated twice.

The dessert is finished by dusting the top layer with generous amounts of unsweetened cocoa powder, providing a slight bitterness that perfectly balances the sweet cream. For the best results, the assembled tiramisu must be refrigerated for at least 6 hours, or ideally overnight, allowing the flavors to meld and the ladyfingers to soften. This chilling process also ensures the dessert sets, making it easy to slice into neat, decadent squares.

Ingredients

  • Savoiardi biscuits
  • Strong Coffee or Espresso
  • cooled Mascarpone Cheese
  • Heavy Whipping Cream
  • Granulated Sugar
  • Vanilla Extract
  • Unsweetened Cocoa Powder

Steps

  1. Prepare Coffee: Brew strong espresso or coffee, add optional coffee liqueur/rum, and let it cool completely.
    2. Make Filling: Beat egg yolks and sugar until thick/pale, then mix in mascarpone cheese until smooth.
  2. Whip Cream: In a separate bowl, whip cold heavy cream to stiff peaks
  3. Combine Filling: Gently fold the whipped cream into the mascarpone mixture until no streaks remain.
  4. Dip Ladyfingers: Briefly dip ladyfingers (savoiardi) into the coffee mixture (1-2 seconds) so they are damp but not soggy.
  5. Assemble Layer 1: Create a single layer of dipped ladyfingers in a baking dish or springform pan.
  6. Add Cream Layer 1: Spread half of the mascarpone cream over the ladyfingers.
  7. Repeat Layers: Repeat with a second layer of dipped ladyfingers and the remaining cream.
  8. Chill: Refrigerate for at least 4-6 hours, or ideally overnight to set.
  9. Dust and Serve: Just before serving, dust generously with cocoa powder.
