Pannacotta Recipe

To prepare a classic vanilla panna cotta, begin by blooming 1 envelope of unflavored gelatin in 2 tablespoons of cold water for about 5 to 10 minutes. In a medium saucepan, combine 2 ½ cups of heavy cream, 1 ½ cups of whole milk (or 1/2 cup for a richer texture), and 1/3 cup of sugar. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar dissolves and the cream is hot, but do not bring it to a boil. Remove from the heat and stir in 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract (or a vanilla bean) and the softened gelatin, whisking until the gelatin is completely dissolved.

Carefully pour the mixture into six individual ramekins or wine glasses, allowing them to cool to room temperature before covering with plastic wrap to prevent a skin from forming. Refrigerate the panna cotta for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight, until it is firmly set but still has a slight wobble. Once set, serve them directly in the glasses or quickly dip the ramekins in hot water to unmold onto plates. Top with fresh berries, fruit coulis, or caramel sauce for a sophisticated dessert.

Ingredients

  • Heavy cream
  • Milk
  • Sugar
  • Unflavoured Gelatin
  • Vanilla Extract

Steps

  1. Bloom Gelatin: Sprinkle 2.5 tsp of gelatin powder over 1/2 cup cold milk in a saucepan and let it sit for 5 to 10 minutes to swell.
  2. Heat Dairy: Add 2 cups heavy cream, 1/4 cup sugar, and a pinch of salt to the saucepan.
  3. Warm over medium-low heat, stirring until the sugar and gelatin are completely dissolved (do not boil).
  4. Add Flavor: Remove from heat and stir in 1-2 tsp vanilla extract.
  5. Pour and Chill: Pour the mixture into 4-6 ramekins or glasses, then refrigerate until fully set—at least 4 hours or overnight.
  6. Serve: Serve directly in the glasses or quickly dip the base of the ramekins in hot water to unmold onto a plate
